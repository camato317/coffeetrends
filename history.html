A timeline of the bean

Did you know?

The word "coffee"

It likely traces from the Arabic qahwa, through Turkish kahve, into Italian caffè — and finally English.

"Cappuccino" comes from monks

Named for the brown robes of Capuchin friars, whose color matched espresso blended with milk.

Penny universities

17th-century English coffeehouses charged a penny entry — and the conversation was so rich they earned this nickname.

Espresso means "pressed"

The Italian term reflects both the pressure used to brew it and that it's made fresh, "expressly," for you.
